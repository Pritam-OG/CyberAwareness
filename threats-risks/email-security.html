✎   Common Email Security Threats

1. Phishing Attacks

In this blog, we will explore the most prevalent email threats and how you can safeguard yourself and your organization.

How to Stay Protected:
  • Verify the sender's email address before clicking on links or downloading attachments.
  • Be cautious of urgent or alarming messages that prompt immediate action.
  • Use email filtering solutions to detect and block phishing attempts.
2. Malware and Ransomware

Malicious attachments or links in emails can install malware or ransomware on your device. Once executed, these programs can steal data, compromise systems, or encrypt files and demand ransom payments.

How to Stay Protected:
  • Do not open suspicious attachments, especially from unknown senders.
  • Keep your operating system and antivirus software updated.
  • Enable automatic scanning of email attachments for malware.
3. Business Email Compromise (BEC)

BEC scams target businesses by impersonating executives or employees. These attacks often involve fraudulent wire transfer requests, fake invoices, or requests for sensitive information.

How to Stay Protected:
  • Confirm financial transactions through direct communication before proceeding.
  • Train employees to recognize social engineering tactics.
  • Implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) for email accounts.
4. Spam and Spoofing

How to Stay Protected:
  • Use email authentication protocols like SPF, DKIM, and DMARC.
  • Configure spam filters to minimize unwanted emails.
  • Never respond to unsolicited emails with personal or financial information.
5. Zero-Day Exploits

Zero-day vulnerabilities refer to software flaws that are unknown to the vendor and are exploited by hackers through email-based attacks before a patch is available.

How to Stay Protected:
  • Keep software and applications up to date to reduce vulnerabilities.
  • Employ advanced email security solutions that use AI-driven threat detection.
  • Report suspicious emails to your IT security team for analysis.
